# Experimental Program for Verification of the Cognitive Shadow Theory  
## (Updated Version, July 2026)

> *"This programme is not a dogma, but a map. Each experiment tests a specific prediction. If the data refute the theory — we will revise the theory. If they confirm it — we will refine the models."*

---

## 1. Conceptual Structure of the Experimental Programme

The cognitive shadow theory predicts effects at three levels:

| Level | Predictions | Tools | Status |
|-------|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Static** (C, S, I, φ) | Differences between clinical groups, correlations with behaviour | EEG, ML classification (GroupKFold) | Partially confirmed: LIS (AUC=0.947), DOC — modest (0.74–0.85), correlations with CRS‑R non‑significant |
| **Dynamic** (R, T, A, F) | Recovery after load, stability over time, metacognitive accuracy, switching flexibility | Mixed models, longitudinal, cognitive tasks | A confirmed (p=0.045), T — only REM sleep, F — requires refinement |
| **Ethical** (Shadow Ethics) | Violation of principles leads to collapse; external support required upon collapse | FORCED_REPORT protocols, interventions | Requires clinical trials |

Experiments are structured according to the principle **from simple to complex** — from basic physiological phenomena to multicentre clinical trials.

## 2. Block 1. Basic Physiological Experiments  
*(Testing consequences of indirect controllability and non‑reproducibility)*

### Experiment 1.1: Breathing Paradox  
**Prediction tested:** Attempting direct volitional control of an automatic interface leads to degradation of its performance (Consequence of indirect controllability, axiom A27.5 — noise dominates under overload).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | In the "breathing control" group, respiratory rhythm variability and subjective discomfort ratings will be significantly higher than in the "free breathing" group (p < 0.01) |
| **Design** | Randomised crossover study: 2 sessions of 20 minutes each (control vs observation), 50 healthy volunteers |
| **Measurements** | Respiratory rhythm (pneumography), heart rate variability (ECG), EEG (theta‑alpha, φ computation), subjective discomfort scales |
| **Key indicator** | Respiratory rhythm variability increase >30% in control group vs <10% in observation group |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 6 (resource redistribution), axiom A27.5 (noise dominance) |
| **Status** | Ready for pilot launch; standard equipment, protocol approved by ethics committee |

---

### Experiment 1.2: Reproducibility of Emotional State  
**Prediction tested:** Impossibility of reproducing an emotional state upon repetition of external conditions (Postulate P1, non‑formalizable remainder).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Upon repeated presentation of emotionally significant stimuli after 7 days, physiological responses will differ significantly from baseline (correlation coefficient <0.6) |
| **Design** | 50 participants, 2 visits with 7‑day interval, presentation of personalised significant stimuli (photos, music) |
| **Measurements** | EEG (spectral complexity, φ), GSR, heart rate, subjective scales (SAM) |
| **Key indicator** | Correlation of physiological patterns between sessions <0.6 with subjective similarity rating >0.8 ("feels different") |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 1′ (incompleteness of representation), Postulate P1 |
| **Status** | Requires ethics committee approval; protocol ready |

## 3. Block 2. Experiments with Altered States of Consciousness  
*(Testing dynamics of φ(refl) and collapse thresholds)*

### Experiment 2.1: LSD and φ_refl Degradation  
**Prediction tested:** LSD induces DMN disintegration, corresponding to a drop in φ_refl below the critical threshold with high risk of metacognitive collapse (Theorem 9).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | LSD administration (100 μg) will cause: (1) DMN disintegration, (2) φ_refl reduction >40%, (3) correlation between φ_refl reduction and subjective reports of "ego dissolution" (r > 0.7) |
| **Design** | Double‑blind placebo‑controlled crossover study, 40 healthy volunteers |
| **Measurements** | fMRI (DMN functional connectivity), MEG (spectral complexity), EEG (alpha power, φ), questionnaires (Ego Dissolution Inventory) |
| **Key indicator** | Correlation between φ_refl reduction and ego‑dissolution intensity >0.7 |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 9, axiom A28 (irreversible degradation) |
| **Risks** | High, requires special authorisation |
| **Status** | Theoretically justified, requires institutional approval |

---

### Experiment 2.2: Psychedelics vs Deep Meditation  
**Prediction tested:** LSD and meditation produce similar neurophysiological patterns, but meditation provides a controlled reduction of φ_refl (with recovery), while LSD provides uncontrolled reduction (with residual deficit).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | LSD and deep meditation demonstrate similar EEG patterns, but (1) the dynamics of φ_refl change differ, (2) subjective controllability is higher in meditation |
| **Design** | 3 groups: LSD (n=15), expert meditators (n=15, practice >10,000 hours), control (n=15) |
| **Measurements** | EEG (gamma coherence, spectral complexity, φ), HRV, subjective scales |
| **Key indicator** | Difference in φ_refl trajectories: meditation — gradual decline with return to baseline, LSD — sharp drop with incomplete recovery (p < 0.01) |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 8 (hysteresis), Theorem 9 (collapse) |
| **Status** | Requires recruitment of expert meditators and institutional approval |

## 4. Block 3. Experiments with Long‑Term Practices  
*(Testing the limit of controllability and the role of external support)*

### Experiment 3.1: Longitudinal Meditation Study  
**Prediction tested:** Long‑term meditation does not guarantee achieving "enlightenment" due to the fundamental limit of controllability (Theorem 9, Principle 0).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Even after 10,000+ hours of practice, only <10% of practitioners show sustained φ_refl >0.8; correlation between practice hours and φ_refl is absent (r < 0.3) |
| **Design** | Cross‑sectional study: 100 practitioners with varying experience (0–50,000 hours), measurement of φ_refl and subjective scales |
| **Measurements** | EEG (spectral complexity, coherence, φ), questionnaires (Five Facet Mindfulness Questionnaire), interviews |
| **Key indicator** | Absence of linear correlation between practice hours and φ_refl (r < 0.3, p > 0.05) |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 9, Principle 0 (incomprehensibility as a gift) |
| **Status** | Requires access to practitioner communities |

---

### Experiment 3.2: Meditation vs Antidepressants  
**Prediction tested:** Recovery of φ_refl in depression is possible only through external support (Principle 3 of Shadow Ethics — duty of external support).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | The "meditation + SSRI" group will show significantly greater φ_refl recovery than the "meditation only" group (difference >0.2, p < 0.01) |
| **Design** | Randomised controlled trial, 120 patients with depression, 12 weeks |
| **Measurements** | EEG (φ_refl, R), clinical scales (HAM‑D, BDI), HRV |
| **Key indicator** | Difference in φ_refl recovery between groups >0.2 (p < 0.01) |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 9, Principle 3 (external support) |
| **Status** | Requires clinical centre and ethics committee approval |

## 5. Block 4. Clinical Experiments  
*(Testing applicability of φ and dynamic parameters in diagnosis and prognosis)*

### Experiment 4.1: FORCED_REPORT in LIS  
**Prediction tested:** The FORCED_REPORT protocol can detect consciousness in LIS patients with accuracy >90% (AUC > 0.9).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | FORCED_REPORT distinguishes LIS from VS with AUC >0.9, sensitivity >90%, specificity >85% |
| **Design** | Prospective multicentre study, N=200 (50 LIS, 50 VS, 50 MCS+, 50 healthy) |
| **Measurements** | Three channels: BCI (mu‑rhythm), pupillary reflex, neurofeedback; computation of φ and dynamic parameters (R, T, A, F) |
| **Key indicator** | AUC >0.9 for LIS vs VS; correlation with CRS‑R >0.6 with synchronous recording |
| **Link to theory** | Principle of signature observability, FORCED_REPORT protocol |
| **Status** | Ready for launch; requires funding and ethical approval |

---

### Experiment 4.2: φ as a Predictor of Recovery  
**Prediction tested:** The value of φ and dynamic parameters (especially R) in the acute phase of DoC predicts recovery at 6 months.

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Patients with φ >0.2 and R >0.05 in the first week have a 3‑fold higher chance of recovery at 6 months |
| **Design** | Prospective cohort study, N=100 patients with DoC, 6‑month follow‑up |
| **Measurements** | EEG (φ, R, T, A, F) on days 1, 3, 7, 14, 30; CRS‑R weekly; outcome at 6 months (CRS‑R, GOS‑E) |
| **Key indicator** | Odds ratio (OR) >3 for φ >0.2 vs φ ≤0.2 (p < 0.01); R as additional predictor |
| **Link to theory** | Theorem 8 (degradation), parameter R (Resilience) |
| **Status** | Requires multicentre coordination |

## 6. Block 5. Instrumental Validation  
*(Comparison with established markers and testing of new measures)*

### Experiment 5.1: Calibration of φ via TMS‑EEG  
**Prediction tested:** φ correlates with Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI) — an established marker of consciousness (Casali et al., 2013).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Correlation between φ and PCI >0.7 across wakefulness, sleep, and anaesthesia |
| **Design** | 30 healthy volunteers, measurements in 4 states (wakefulness, sleep, propofol anaesthesia, ketamine) |
| **Measurements** | TMS‑EEG (PCI), spontaneous EEG (φ, T), behavioural scales |
| **Key indicator** | φ‑PCI correlation >0.7 across all states (p < 0.001) |
| **Link to theory** | Principle of signature observability, convergent validity |
| **Status** | Requires TMS setup and anaesthesiologist |

---

### Experiment 5.2: Validation of R, T, A, F  
**Prediction tested:** New dynamic measures (Resilience, Temporal Coherence, Awareness, Flexibility) differentiate clinical groups and complement static φ.

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | R,T,A,F significantly differentiate healthy, chronic stress patients, and MCS− (p < 0.05 after Bonferroni correction) |
| **Design** | According to protocols from `Empirical_Protocols_R_T_A_F.md`: N=50 healthy, N=30 chronic stress, N=20 MCS− |
| **Measurements** | meta‑d′ (metacognitive accuracy), EEG (φ(t)), confidence ratings, load tasks |
| **Key indicator** | Pairwise group differences significant (p < 0.05); A already confirmed (p=0.045), T and F require refinement |
| **Link to theory** | Second‑order model extension, dynamic parameters |
| **Status** | Protocols ready; requires participant recruitment; partial data already obtained (A confirmed) |

---

### Experiment 5.3: Cross-population validation of φ on clinical and physiological datasets

**Prediction tested:** φ generalises across clinical datasets (DOC, CLIS) but does not transfer to physiological changes (sleep, anaesthesia).

**Design:** Retrospective analysis with training on one dataset and testing on another without tuning. Used Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, logistic regression, SVM. Applied SMOTE for balancing. Evaluated AUC.

**Results (obtained):** AUC = 0.887 for DOC → CLIS (Random Forest + SMOTE). All CAP‑related results were near chance (0.47–0.56). Detailed tables and interpretation are included in the main article (Section 3.4.1).

**Falsification criterion:** If AUC for DOC → CLIS < 0.75 or AUC for CAP > 0.6, the hypothesis of generalisability and specificity is not confirmed.

**Status:** ✅ Completed (retrospective analysis). Prospective validation requires new data collection on independent cohorts.

**Link to theory:** Results are consistent with Theorem 10 (signature observability) — AUC never reaches 1.0, and reconstruction accuracy depends on the domain from which signatures are drawn.

## 9. Criteria for Success and Falsification

### 9.1. Criteria for Theory Confirmation

| Level | Criterion |
|-------|-----------|
| **Static** | Experiment 4.1: AUC >0.9 for LIS vs VS; Experiment 5.1: φ‑PCI correlation >0.7 |
| **Dynamic** | Experiment 5.2: R,T,A,F significantly differentiate clinical groups (p < 0.05); A already confirmed |
| **Ethical** | Experiment 3.2: φ_refl recovery requires external support (difference >0.2, p < 0.01) |

### 9.2. Falsification Criteria (What Would Refute the Theory)

| Prediction | What refutes it |
|------------|-----------------|
| Indirect controllability (1.1) | No differences between control and observation groups (p > 0.05) |
| Emotional non‑reproducibility (1.2) | Pattern correlation between sessions >0.8 with subjective similarity >0.8 |
| LSD collapse (2.1) | Absence of significant φ_refl drop or absence of correlation with ego‑dissolution |
| Limit of controllability (3.1) | Linear correlation between practice hours and φ_refl >0.5 |
| External support (3.2) | "Meditation only" group shows the same recovery as SSRI group |
| FORCED_REPORT (4.1) | AUC <0.8 for LIS vs VS |
| R,T,A,F (5.2) | Absence of significant group differences (p > 0.05) |
