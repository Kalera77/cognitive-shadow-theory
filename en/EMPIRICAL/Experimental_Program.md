# Experimental Program for Verifying the Cognitive Shadow Theory  
## (Updated Version, July 2026)

> *"This program is not a dogma but a map. Each experiment tests a specific prediction. If the data refute the theory — we revise the theory. If they confirm it — we refine the models."*

---

## 1. Conceptual Structure of the Experimental Program

The Cognitive Shadow Theory predicts effects at three levels:

| Level | Predictions | Tools | Status |
|-------|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Static** (C, S, I, φ) | Differences between clinical groups, correlations with behavior | EEG, ML classification (GroupKFold) | Partially confirmed: LIS (AUC=0.947), DOC — modest (0.74–0.85), correlations with CRS‑R non‑significant |
| **Dynamic** (R, T, A, F) | Recovery after load, temporal stability, metacognitive accuracy, flexibility | Mixed models, longitudinal, cognitive tasks | A confirmed (p=0.045), T — only REM sleep, F — needs refinement |
| **Ethical** (Shadow Ethics) | Violation of principles leads to collapse; external support required at collapse | FORCED_REPORT protocols, interventions | Requires clinical trials |

Experiments are ordered from simple to complex — from basic physiological phenomena to multicenter clinical trials.

## 2. Block 1. Basic Physiological Experiments  
*(Testing corollaries about indirect controllability and non‑reproducibility)*

### Experiment 1.1: Breathing Paradox  
**Prediction tested:** Attempting direct voluntary control of an automatic interface degrades its performance (Corollary on indirect controllability, axiom A27.5 — noise dominates under overload).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | In the "breathing control" group, respiratory rhythm variability and subjective discomfort ratings will be significantly higher than in the "free breathing" group (p < 0.01) |
| **Design** | Randomized crossover: 2 sessions of 20 min each (control vs observation), 50 healthy volunteers |
| **Measurements** | Respiratory rhythm (pneumography), heart rate variability (ECG), EEG (theta‑alpha, φ), subjective discomfort scales |
| **Key indicator** | Increase in respiratory variability >30% in control group vs <10% in observation group |
| **Theory link** | Theorem 6 (resource redistribution), axiom A27.5 (noise dominance) |
| **Status** | Ready for pilot launch; standard equipment, protocol approved by ethics committee |

---

### Experiment 1.2: Reproducibility of Emotional State  
**Prediction tested:** Impossibility to reproduce an emotional state when external conditions are repeated (Postulate P1, non‑formalizable remainder).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Upon re‑exposure to emotionally significant stimuli after 7 days, physiological responses will differ significantly from baseline (correlation coefficient <0.6) |
| **Design** | 50 participants, 2 visits with 7‑day interval, presentation of personalized significant stimuli (photos, music) |
| **Measurements** | EEG (spectral complexity, φ), GSR, HR, subjective scales (SAM) |
| **Key indicator** | Correlation of physiological patterns between sessions <0.6, while subjective similarity >0.8 ("feels different") |
| **Theory link** | Theorem 1′ (incompleteness of representation), Postulate P1 |
| **Status** | Requires ethics approval; protocol ready |

## 3. Block 2. Long‑Term Practices and External Support  
*(Testing limits of controllability and the role of external support)*

### Experiment 2.1: Longitudinal Meditation  
**Prediction tested:** Long‑term meditation does not guarantee achieving "enlightenment" due to a fundamental limit of controllability (Theorem 9, Principle 0).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Even after 10,000+ hours of practice, fewer than 10% of practitioners show sustained φ_refl >0.8; correlation between practice hours and φ_refl is absent (r < 0.3) |
| **Design** | Cross‑sectional: 100 practitioners with varying experience (0–50,000 hours), measurement of φ_refl and subjective scales |
| **Measurements** | EEG (spectral complexity, coherence, φ), questionnaires (Five Facet Mindfulness Questionnaire), interviews |
| **Key indicator** | Absence of linear correlation between practice hours and φ_refl (r < 0.3, p > 0.05) |
| **Theory link** | Theorem 9, Principle 0 (incomprehensibility as a gift) |
| **Status** | Requires access to meditation communities |

---

### Experiment 2.2: Meditation vs Antidepressants  
**Prediction tested:** Recovery of φ_refl in depression is possible only through external support (Principle 3 of Shadow Ethics — obligation of external support).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | The "meditation + SSRI" group will show significantly greater recovery of φ_refl than the "meditation only" group (difference >0.2, p < 0.01) |
| **Design** | Randomized controlled trial, 120 patients with depression, 12 weeks |
| **Measurements** | EEG (φ_refl, R), clinical scales (HAM‑D, BDI), HRV |
| **Key indicator** | Difference in φ_refl recovery between groups >0.2 (p < 0.01) |
| **Theory link** | Theorem 9, Principle 3 (external support) |
| **Status** | Requires clinical center and ethics approval |

## 4. Block 3. Clinical Experiments  
*(Testing applicability of φ and dynamic parameters in diagnosis and prognosis)*

### Experiment 3.1: FORCED_REPORT for LIS  
**Prediction tested:** The FORCED_REPORT protocol detects consciousness in LIS patients with accuracy >90% (AUC > 0.9).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | FORCED_REPORT distinguishes LIS from VS with AUC >0.9, sensitivity >90%, specificity >85% |
| **Design** | Prospective multicenter study, N=200 (50 LIS, 50 VS, 50 MCS+, 50 healthy) |
| **Measurements** | Three channels: BCI (mu‑rhythm), pupillary reflex, neurofeedback; compute φ and dynamic parameters (R, T, A, F) |
| **Key indicator** | AUC >0.9 for LIS vs VS; correlation with CRS‑R >0.6 during simultaneous recording |
| **Theory link** | Principle of observability of signatures, FORCED_REPORT protocol |
| **Status** | Ready to launch; requires funding and ethics approval |

---

### Experiment 3.2: φ as a Predictor of Recovery  
**Prediction tested:** The value of φ and dynamic parameters (especially R) in the acute phase of DoC predicts recovery at 6 months.

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Patients with φ >0.2 and R >0.05 in the first week have a 3‑fold higher chance of recovery at 6 months |
| **Design** | Prospective cohort study, N=100 patients with DoC, 6‑month follow‑up |
| **Measurements** | EEG (φ, R, T, A, F) on days 1, 3, 7, 14, 30; CRS‑R weekly; outcome at 6 months (CRS‑R, GOS‑E) |
| **Key indicator** | Odds ratio (OR) >3 for φ >0.2 vs φ ≤0.2 (p < 0.01); R as an additional predictor |
| **Theory link** | Theorem 8 (degradation), parameter R (Resilience) |
| **Status** | Requires multi‑center coordination |

## 5. Block 4. Instrumental Validation  
*(Comparison with established markers and testing new measurements)*

### Experiment 4.1: Calibration of φ via TMS‑EEG  
**Prediction tested:** φ correlates with Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI) — an established marker of consciousness (Casali et al., 2013).

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Correlation between φ and PCI >0.7 in wakefulness, sleep, and anesthesia |
| **Design** | 30 healthy volunteers, measurements in 4 states (wake, sleep, propofol, ketamine) |
| **Measurements** | TMS‑EEG (PCI), spontaneous EEG (φ, T), behavioral scales |
| **Key indicator** | Correlation φ‑PCI >0.7 across all states (p < 0.001) |
| **Theory link** | Principle of observability of signatures, convergent validity |
| **Status** | Requires TMS setup and anesthesiologist |

---

### Experiment 4.2: Validation of R, T, A, F  
**Prediction tested:** New dynamic measures (Resilience, Temporal Coherence, Awareness, Flexibility) distinguish clinical groups and complement static φ.

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | R, T, A, F significantly differentiate healthy subjects, chronic stress patients, and MCS− (p < 0.05 after Bonferroni correction) |
| **Design** | According to protocols from `Empirical_Protocols_R_T_A_F.md`: N=50 healthy, N=30 chronic stress, N=20 MCS− |
| **Measurements** | meta‑d' (metacognitive accuracy), EEG (φ(t)), confidence ratings, load tasks |
| **Key indicator** | Pairwise group differences significant (p < 0.05); A already confirmed (p=0.045), T and F need refinement |
| **Theory link** | Extension of the 2nd‑order model, dynamic parameters |
| **Status** | Protocols ready; requires participant recruitment; partial data already obtained (A confirmed) |

---

### Experiment 4.3: Cross‑Population Validation of φ on Clinical and Physiological Datasets  
**Prediction tested:** φ generalizes across clinical datasets (DOC, CLIS), but does not transfer to physiological changes (sleep, anesthesia).

**Design:** Retrospective analysis with training on one dataset and testing on another without fine‑tuning. Used Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, logistic regression, SVM. SMOTE applied for balancing. AUC evaluated.

**Results (obtained):** AUC = 0.887 for DOC → CLIS (Random Forest + SMOTE). All AUCs involving CAP (sleep) were in the 0.47–0.56 range. Detailed tables and interpretation are included in the main article (Section 3.4.1).

**Falsification criterion:** If AUC for DOC → CLIS < 0.75 or AUC for CAP > 0.6, the hypothesis of generalizability and specificity of φ is not supported.

**Status:** ✅ Completed (retrospective analysis). Prospective validation requires new data collection on independent cohorts.

**Theory link:** Results are consistent with Theorem 10 (principle of observability of signatures) — AUC never reaches 1.0, and reconstruction accuracy depends on the domain from which signatures are drawn.

## 8. Success and Falsification Criteria

### 8.1. Criteria for Confirming the Theory

| Level | Criterion |
|-------|-----------|
| **Static** | Experiment 3.1: AUC >0.9 for LIS vs VS; Experiment 4.1: φ‑PCI correlation >0.7 |
| **Dynamic** | Experiment 4.2: R,T,A,F significantly differentiate clinical groups (p < 0.05); A already confirmed |
| **Ethical** | Experiment 2.2: φ_refl recovery requires external support (difference >0.2, p < 0.01) |

### 8.2. Falsification Criteria (what would refute the theory)

| Prediction | What would refute it |
|------------|----------------------|
| Indirect controllability (1.1) | No difference between control and observation groups (p > 0.05) |
| Non‑reproducibility of emotions (1.2) | Pattern correlation between sessions >0.8 with subjective similarity >0.8 |
| Limit of controllability (2.1) | Linear correlation between practice hours and φ_refl >0.5 |
| External support (2.2) | "Meditation only" group shows same recovery as SSRI group |
| FORCED_REPORT (3.1) | AUC <0.8 for LIS vs VS |
| R,T,A,F (4.2) | No significant differences between groups (p > 0.05) |
